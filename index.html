Engineering

Rail Infrastructure
at Scale

Fifteen years in rail means knowing what it costs when a grade crossing goes wrong, what it takes to get a Class I railroad to say yes, and how to deliver complex infrastructure when everyone's schedule conflicts. I run Wilson & Company's West Coast rail practice: freight, passenger, and the politics in between.

01

Freight & Passenger Rail

Design and delivery of complex rail projects across the Western United States: track geometry, grade separations, double-track programs, and intermodal facilities.

02

Client Relationships

Deep working relationships with Class I railroads and transit agencies. Bridging the gap between public agency timelines and railroad operational realities.

03

Business Development

Proposal strategy, pursuit leadership, and contract negotiations on infrastructure contracts from preliminary engineering through final design.

04

Alternative Delivery

Design-build, CM/GC, and progressive design-build procurement. Coordinating multi-discipline teams through fast-track project delivery.
